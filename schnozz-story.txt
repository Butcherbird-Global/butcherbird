# They Don't Call Them Nasal Strips Anymore

**How we launched a brand so hard that the product became the category. R4 million in revenue in the first three months.**

---

## The Brand

Schnozz makes premium silk nasal strips. Better breathing, better sleep, better performance. Simple product. But in a market where most people didn't even know nasal strips existed beyond the cheap pharmacy ones, Schnozz had a bigger job than selling — they had to create the category.

## The Launch

Most brands launch with a landing page and a prayer. Schnozz launched with an army.

We assembled over 300 ambassadors before a single ad was ever placed. Athletes, wellness creators, sleep coaches, moms, biohackers — anyone with an audience and a nose. The brief was simple: try them, film it, post it.

The result was over 2 million organic views before we spent a single rand on media. Schnozz wasn't an ad in someone's feed. It was a recommendation from someone they trusted. By the time we turned on paid, people were already searching for it.

## The Paid Media Push

With organic proof-of-concept in hand, we went aggressive.

Full-funnel campaign structure from day one. Prospecting creative built from the best-performing ambassador content — real faces, real reactions, real results. Not a single stock photo. Not a single scripted testimonial.

We let the data decide what scaled. A testimonial video shot on the Sea Point promenade hit 6.62x ROAS. A static ad about hayfever peaked at 8.60x in a single day. A kids' mouth breathing angle — something no competitor had even thought of — hit 7.37x.

Every week, we tested new angles. Every week, the winners got more budget. Every week, the losers got cut.

## What Happened

- **R4 million+ in revenue** in the first three months
- **8.20x ROAS** in the strongest week — 402 purchases
- **2,133 purchases in a single month**
- First-to-market with a **kids nasal strip line** in South Africa

But the numbers only tell half the story.

## The Real Win

Schnozz didn't just sell strips. Schnozz became the word.

People stopped saying "nasal strips." They started saying "Schnozz strips" — the same way people say "Uber" instead of "ride" or "Google" instead of "search." When your brand name replaces the generic term, you've won something advertising can't buy.

That kind of cultural penetration opened every door. Amazon. Takealot. Wellness Warehouse. Sportsman's Warehouse. Mr Price Sport.

Today, Schnozz is the number one brand in the category in South Africa. And they're not stopping — US expansion is already underway.

It started with 300 ambassadors and a bet that if enough people tried it, the product would sell itself.

The product did its job. We just made sure the right people saw it.
